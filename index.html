Les Paul

The Wizard of Waukesha

Les Paul's Career in Music:

  • Les Paul born Lester Polsfuss 1915-2009 in Waukesha, Wisconsin
  • Called “the Wizard of Waukesha” for his inventions.
  • His teacher told his parents: “Your son, Lester, will never learn music.”
  • Taught himself to play harmonica, guitar and banjo.
  • 1930’s-Performs regularly in Country Western and Jazz bands in St. Louis and Chicago.
  • Modifies an acoustic guitar using a phonograph needle to amplify the guitar.
  • 1941-Designs “The Log”, a solid body electric guitar. The instrument was essentially a 4 x 4 piece of wood. Paul brings his invention to the Gibson Guitar company who reject it for 10 years until Leo Fender designs a solid body electric guitar of his own.
  • 1943-While serving with the Armed Forces Radio Network, accompanies such artists as Bing Crosby, Kate Smith and Nat King Cole.
  • 1945-Les Paul builds a home recording studio where he develops multi-track recording and experiments with sound effects including tape delay, flanging, phasing and overdubbing enabling him to play multiple instruments simultaneously on one recording, techniques that are still in use today.
  • 1948-Car accident shatters his right arm and elbow. Les Paul asks doctors to set his arm at a 90 degree angle so that he can continue to play guitar.
  • 1949-Marriage to Mary Ford. Recording as a duo, Les Paul’s multi-track techniques are used to double the voice and instrument tracks on the recordings.
  • How High the Moon-Les Paul and Mary Ford:
    • How High the Moon.
  • Les Paul is the only person to be inducted into both the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame and the National Inventors Hall of Fame.
